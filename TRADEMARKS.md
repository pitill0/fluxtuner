## Allowed uses

You may use the FluxTuner name for accurate, non-confusing references such as:

- describing that your work is a fork of FluxTuner;
- linking to the official FluxTuner repository;
- saying that software is compatible with FluxTuner;
- preserving attribution notices required by the applicable software license.

## Not allowed without permission

Without prior written permission, you may not:

- present a fork, hosted service, SaaS, package, build, or derivative work as
  official FluxTuner;
- use names such as "FluxTuner Cloud", "FluxTuner Hosted", "FluxTuner Pro",
  "Official FluxTuner", or similar names that imply endorsement or affiliation;
- use the FluxTuner logo, icon, screenshots, or branding for a commercial
  hosted service or paid product;
- register domains, social accounts, app listings, packages, or services that
  imply official ownership of the FluxTuner project;
- use FluxTuner branding in a way that confuses users about who operates a
  service or who is responsible for support, privacy, security, or billing.

## Hosted services and forks

Forks and hosted services must make their independent status clear. If a service
uses FluxTuner code, it should use its own name and branding, and it must not
imply that it is operated, endorsed, supported, or secured by the official
FluxTuner project unless permission has been granted.

## Commercial permission

Commercial use of the FluxTuner brand requires prior written permission from the
FluxTuner maintainer or another authorized rights holder.
